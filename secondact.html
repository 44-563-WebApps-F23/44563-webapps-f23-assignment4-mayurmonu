MRS.ALVING.

[Still in the doorway.]

_Velbekomme_ Mr. Manders. Aren't you coming too, Oswald?

OSWALD.

[From within.]

No, thank you. I think I shall go out a little.

MRS.ALVING.

Yes, do. The weather seems a little brighter now.

[She shuts the dining-room door, goes to the hall door, and c alls:] Regina!

REGINA.

[Outside.]

, Mrs. Alving?

MRS.ALVING.

Go down to the laundry, and help with the garlands.

REGINA.

Yes, Mrs. Alving.

[MRS. ALVING assures herself that REGINA goes; then shuts the door.]
